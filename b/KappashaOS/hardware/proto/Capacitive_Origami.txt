## Abstract
A self-folding, porosity-tapered ribbon battery with origami pre-crease etch, dual-axis mandrel wrapping, and gap control via etched channel thickness. Designed to maximise anode-cathode breathing space inside fixed-volume casings.

## Descriptions
- A thin-film ribbon battery comprising: a) anode and cathode foils laminated with a dielectric separator; b) laser-etched valley channels 0.2–0.4 mm wide along pre-programmed fold lines; c) tapered porosity gradient from dense (base) to light (tip) via variable etch depth; d) dual-motion mandrel that rotates and translates to wrap ribbon into helical petals; e) flat-to-3D surface ratio > 4 : 1 in final form.
- The battery of claim 1 wherein creases are masked with 10 µm photoresist, etched in 5 % HF, folded to 45° valley / 135° mountain pattern, then sealed with epoxy under vacuum.
- A method: extrude 0.8 mm ribbon → etch → fold → wrap → cap. No solvents after wrap.
- Capacitance > 20 % above planar equivalent, zero dendrite bridging for 5 000 cycles.
- Open-source hardware licence attached: Apache 2.0, no royalties, no reverse-fees.
- Porosity control via angular etch (45° relative to ribbon plane). Valley channel not flat — it's angled. So when you fold, the crease isn't just thin — it's funnel-shaped. Dendrites get lost in the angle. Gaps become tunnels. You don't roll porosity. You sculpt it. You don't manage dendrite growth. You redirect it. It's not random. It's geometry. It's like building stairs so the water doesn't flood. Etch the 45°. Fold the river.
